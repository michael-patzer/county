County Lookup: Search and Browse All 3,000+ US Counties

The US has over 3,000 counties and county-equivalents. County Names & Lines lets you search, browse, and view any of them — finding county boundaries, seats, and information for any part of the country.

01

Browse counties by state

Open the County Browser and select any state to see all of its counties listed alphabetically with their county seats and population data. Tap any county to see it on the map with its full boundary highlighted.

All 50 states, the District of Columbia, and US territories (Puerto Rico, Guam, US Virgin Islands, etc.) are covered, with their county-equivalent administrative divisions included.

02

Search counties by name

Type any county name in the search bar to find it instantly. Many county names appear in multiple states — there are over 30 counties named "Jefferson" and over 25 named "Lincoln" across the US. Search results show all matching counties with their state, letting you select the specific one you need.

The search also matches county seats — searching "Springfield" will find all counties whose seat is Springfield, not just counties named Springfield.

03

FIPS codes and administrative data

Every county in the US has a 5-digit FIPS code — a federal numeric identifier. The first two digits identify the state; the last three identify the county within the state. FIPS codes are required for federal data systems, grant applications, regulatory filings, and statistical analysis.

The app shows the FIPS code for every county in its information panel. Copy the code with a single tap for use in other applications.

04

County equivalents and special territories

Not every state calls its primary administrative division a "county." Louisiana has parishes; Alaska has boroughs and census areas; Virginia has independent cities that are county-equivalents. The app correctly labels and displays all of these using their official designations rather than forcing them all into a "county" label.

This is important for accurate administrative filings — referring to a Louisiana parish as a county, for example, can cause document processing issues.

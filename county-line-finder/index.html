Find County Lines and See Where Boundaries Run

County boundaries often run through the middle of roads, fields, waterways, and neighborhoods — and they're rarely marked on the ground. County Names & Lines overlays every US county boundary on an interactive map so you can see exactly where the lines are.

01

Why county boundaries matter on the ground

County lines have practical implications that aren't always obvious: property tax jurisdiction, hunting and fishing regulations, speed limits on unincorporated roads, school district assignment, and emergency service response zones can all change at the county line.

Knowing where the line is — especially in rural areas where there's no visible marker — is useful for hunters, landowners, surveyors, real estate professionals, and anyone doing business across county lines.

02

How to find a county line near you

Open the map and zoom in to your area of interest. County boundaries appear as colored lines on the map. Zoom in further to see the precise path of the boundary — which side of a road it runs on, whether it follows a waterway or a surveyed line, and how it intersects with other boundaries.

Tap the boundary line itself to see the names of the counties on each side and the state the boundary is in.

03

County lines that follow natural features

Many county boundaries follow rivers, ridgelines, and watershed boundaries — particularly in older states where the original surveys used natural features as reference points. Other boundaries follow straight surveyed lines regardless of natural features.

The app's map shows the actual boundary path overlaid on a satellite or topo base map, making it easy to see the relationship between the county line and the physical landscape.

04

Tracking boundary crossings in real time

With location tracking active, the app shows your position moving relative to county lines in real time. As you approach a county line while driving or hiking, you can see exactly how close you are and which county you're about to enter.

A notification option alerts you when you cross into a new county — useful for keeping track of county-level license requirements or documenting which counties you've passed through.
